Jon Terry

Jon has been a theatre actor for many years and has appeared in numerous productions including Waiting for Godot, The 39 Steps and many Alan Ayckbourn productions.

Following lockdown Jon is planning to once again make acting his main focus and has a number of exciting projects lined up.

Helena Jevons

Helena cut her acting teeth as a teenager playing small feminine roles like Macbeth and Henry Higgins at an all girls’ grammar school. She was hard to keep off stages after that although she does like to occasionally play an actual woman - her favourite role ever was madcap Bobbie in Neil Simon’s Last of the Red Hot Lovers.

She lives in seaford with her two young children, plays guitar pretty badly and salsas pretty well.

This is her first acting gig post motherhood and she hopes she can remember what she is doing.

To pay the bills she consults with local businesses from the University Business Incubator at Sussex University at Falmer and builds websites for local start ups.

Verity Terry

Georgina graduated from Arts Educational Schools London in 2015.

She has spent the past 3 years working for Immersive Theatre Company, Excellent Experiences; with company credits including Rose DeWitt Bukater in ‘The Titanic Experience’, Ghost of Christmas Past in ‘A Christmas Carol’ and Rose Mylett in ‘Jack the Ripper Live’.

Other recent credits include; Countess Olivia in 'Twelfth Night', (Curzon Theatre Productions) Theodora in ‘The Haunting of Hill House’ (Curzon Theatre Productions), Elizabeth in ‘Vindication Swim’ (Relash Films) Emma in ‘Truth Will Out’ (Troubadour Theatre,) Sarah in ‘Hidden Agenda’ (SONY) and Grace in ‘The Rest of Us’ (SKY Arts).

Georgina Robinson

Since completing Drama GCSE and achieving a 9, Verity is now studying Drama and Theatre Studies A-level at Eastbourne College.

She has been in several school productions, most recently ‘Les Miserables’ and now she’s about to act in ‘The Audience’ by Michael Frayn.

Furthermore, she has designed a website for a theatre company, which was well received and she still manages it.

She also has just completed the highest grade (grade 8) in LAMDA Acting Performance Examinations and is hoping for a distinction.’
